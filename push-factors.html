Teaching in Asia ✈

Tips when moving with a family

Push Factors

When I decided to apply, the push factors were likely be familiar to many teachers, especially those with a young family: feeling totally exhausted at the end of another week teaching in a school with a class of 30+ students. With having three young children at home, money was tight. Nights out were few and far between. When the weekend did come round, work pervaded. The soothing light at the end of the tunnel is just a freight train coming your way; don’t worry, when you’re 68 and the kids of the kids you teach have had kids, you might finally get to draw your pension. But, at the moment, you’re just a little more in debt every month and feeling as if something might snap. The long-term effects of these factors can take their toll.
