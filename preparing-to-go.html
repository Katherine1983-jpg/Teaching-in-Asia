Teaching in Asia ✈

Tips when moving with a family

Preparing to go

  • VISAs

If you are satisfied that your new employer will offer a good salary, after living costs, and that they will offer a good standard of education for your children, it’s time to get organised. The school’s admin team should send you information about the VISA process and more recently, vaccine and quarantine requirements. Every International School I’ve worked with has been good at this but I’d still recommend asking questions if you’re unsure about something. Depending on the country, the VISA process might require multiple trips to the embassy. If you live a long way from the embassy, this can be a real problem. There are companies, who will, for a fee, sort this out for you. I’ve only used a VISA company once, but they were very efficient and took the majority of the hassle out of getting five of us to China. If you accepted an offer late in the academic year and there’s not much time before you leave (perhaps three or four months), this might be a good option for you.

  • Apps

If you’ve been ‘buddied up’ with a teacher at your new school before you go, ask them about Apps they recommend. Depending on where you’re going, there will be apps that everyone uses for communication, transport, banking, food delivery, and making payments and you’ll be using most of them the first day you arrive!

  • Shipping

The first two occasions that I travelled with my family, we did so with just a suitcase and carry on. This is very achievable and I the option I prefer. Shipping can be costly in terms of time and money and is generally worth avoiding if possible. Having said that, I have used a shipping companies for things like bicycles, my children’s books, sentimental things and a few small pieces of furniture. If you want to ship, get quotes from as many different companies as possible as their prices can really vary. As the items I shipped were inconvenient to replace rather than valuable, I opted out of the insurance which saved me about 20% of what the cost would have been.
