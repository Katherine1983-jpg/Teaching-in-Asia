Tips when moving with a family

Returning home

Deciding to return home can bring up a plethora of mixed emotions. Some of the very best teachers I’ve met while abroad were really looking forward to returning to the rigor and professionalism that is characteristic of state schools in their home countries but were worried about the nosedive their personal finances would take. As I’d set out a strict saving plan, I was happy that I’d paid off money I’d borrowed for my wedding and had no remaining credit card debt…for the first time since I was 18! My husband had managed to clear his credit cards and even managed to pay off his student loan. As debt was a big cause of stress for my family, we felt, despite having limited savings, we were in a much better position to enjoy life in the U.K. My three children were also keen to go home – we’d promised them we’d settle in one place until they were done with school! They’ve experienced different cultures, lived in some fantastic places and enjoyed holidays we could have never afforded if we’d had stayed at home. Once they’re grown, I may be tempted to try the international game once again.
